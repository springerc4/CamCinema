The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014)

Gross Revenue: $172.90 million

Ratings:

8.1 / 10

92%

Director

Wes Anderson

Stars

Ralph Fiennes, Adrien Brody, Willem Dafoe, Tony Revolori

Synopsis

The concierge of a famous European hotel, M. Gustave, hires a new lobby boy named Zero and takes him through his past when the hotel was in its golden age. While in the present, a family of a recently deceased resident of the hotel arrives to claim what they believe will be bequeathed to one of them, but things turn messy quickly.

Why it's here

Wes Anderson certainly has his own way of making movies, and its a style that I personally love and one that has garnered him plenty of fans like me. The Grand Budapest Hotel is all sorts of genres wrapped into one but this film is an adventure to me, the viewer, since it is so stylish and up-tempo. Don't get me wrong, though, because this movie is good for much more than just thrills and laughs.




Lawrence of Arabia (1962)

Gross Revenue: $70 million

Ratings:

8.3 / 10

97%

Director

David Lean

Stars

Peter O'Toole, Alec Guiness, Jack Hawkins

Synopsis

T.E. Lawrence, a lieutenant of the British military is sent to Arabia to unite the Arab tribes in their fight against the Turks during WW1.

Why it's here

The cinematography is a treat to look at for the mesmerizing four hours of movie the viewer has to revel in. I wasn't aware of how great this movie was upon my first viewing, simply because I wasn't accustomed to watching epics at the time. As I began to understand the enormous accomplishment that this movie was, however, each viewing after that was an entirely fresh experience.

Director

Synopsis

A vigilante wearing a Guy Fawkes mask known only as "V", plots to overthrow a fascist government set in a future Britain. A young girl named Evey Hammond becomes entangled in this plot and is forced to give up everything to help him overthrow tyranny.

Why it's here

Of all the movies I've seen this past year, V for Vendetta has one of the most entertaining endings. It is an endlessly relevant tale that never fails to entertain.
