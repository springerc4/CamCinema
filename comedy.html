The Big Lebowski (1998)

Gross Revenue: $46.70 million

Ratings:

8.1 / 10

83%

Directors

Ethan Coen, Joel Coen

Stars

Jeff Bridges, John Goodman, Julianna Moore, Steve Buscemi

Synopsis

Through a series of unfortunate misunderstandings and miscommunications, Jeffrey Lebowski, better known as 'The Dude' is entangled in a mysterious kidnapping scheme. His character, however, does not fit the detective type, so the story unfolds in a manner similar to his own lackadaisical endeavors.

Why it's here

This movie is not made to be entirely understood on its first viewing, but every scene with a foot in the bowling alley is immediately iconic, and the direction of the Coen brothers makes this a hilariously unpredicatable movie filled with witty dialogue.

Modern Times (1936)

Gross Revenue: $1.8 million

Ratings:

8.5 / 10

100%

Director

Charlie Chaplin

Stars

Charlie Chaplin, Paulette Goddard

Synopsis

Charlie Chaplin's "tramp" character struggles to adjust to the modern world in all aspects of his life, from his job to his misadventures with the law.

Why it's here

Chaplin was one of the finest filmakers of the early to mid-twentieth century. His style of slapstick comedy influenced many who came after him, yet his own work continues to amuse audiences nearly a century after his prime. Modern Times was my introduction to Chaplin and was therefore my introduction to an integral part of cinematic history.
