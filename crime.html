Gross Revenue: $83.60 million

Ratings:

8.3 / 10

73%

Director

Guy Ritchie

Stars

Benicio Del Toro, Dennis Farina, Jason Statham, Brad Pitt

Synopsis

A racket of illegal boxing and stolen jewelry brings together a mix of incompetent criminals and dangerous professionals in this energetic caper.

Why it's here

This falls into my crime comedy sub-genre and could've easily been a part of my comedy list. The story, although filled with dark humor, brings together a great ensemble cast and intertwines multiple sub-plots effortlessly with a satisfying ending, making it more than a comedy for me.




A Clockwork Orange (1971)

Gross Revenue: $26.60 million

Ratings:

8.3 / 10

83%

Director

Stanley Kubrick

Stars

Malcolm Mcdowell, Patrick Magee, Warren Clarke

Synopsis

Alex DeLarge, a troubled and extremely vicious sociopath roams the nights with his droogs always in the mood for a bit of "ultra-violence". When his crimes catch up with him, Alex undergoes a new treatment meant to cure him of his violent tendencies.

Why it's here

Controversial upon its release, A Clockwork Orange adapts one of my favorite books into a visually appealing, yet terrifying, dystopian future. The Nadsat argot the novel was written in made this story one of the most memorable I've read and seen on screen. Just like all of Stanley Kubrick's films, you can watch hundreds of times and still find something new every time and develop your next big theory.

Ratings:

Director

Dan Gilroy

Stars

Jake Gyllenhaal, Rene Russo, Riz Ahmed

Synopsis

Louis Bloom, a convincing but clearly unstable con man, gets a job as a crime journalist in LA. Desperate for stories, he creeps a little too close to the crimes he reports.

Why it's here

Jake Gyllenhaal delivers one of the better performances I've seen in a film to date. This film gives an uncomfortable but captivating look into the state of privacy and the fascination we have with other people's lives.
