Foreign Films

*Foreign films are not a separate genre, but I will set them apart because some viewers don't prefer to read subtitles


The Intouchables (2011)

Gross Revenue: 9.5 Million EUR

Ratings:

8.5 / 10

75%

Directors

Olivier Nakache, Eric Toledano

Stars

Omar Sy, Francois Cluzet, Anne Le Ny

Synopsis

Seemingly opposite characters, Philippe and Driss, come together in an unexpected way to forge a strong friendship. Philippe, a wealthy aristocratic quadriplegic, hires Driss, a young man from the projects, to be his caregiver. Despite Driss's lack of qualifications, the two become very close friends as they quickly get accustomed to each other.

Why it's here

This is a classic "opposites attract" story that might offend some viewers if they are disgusted by some outdated cliches, but the movie is a fun time nonetheless. There is a very heartwarming dyanmic between the two lead actors, and it leads to plenty of lighthearted dialogue/moments between the two.




El Secreto de Sus Ojos (2009)

Gross Revenue: $34 million

Ratings:

8.2 / 10

89%

Director

Juan Jose Campanella

Stars

Ricardo Darin, Soledad Villamil, Pablo Rago, Javier Godino

Synopsis

Retired Aregntinian federal justice officer looks to find closure for an unsolved case that still haunts him decades later. Esposito juggles his long unrequited love for his co-worker Irene, with his investigation of the brutal rape and murder case from decades ago. Esposito learns valuable lessons about passion, grief, and love through his revisitation of the key players in the case.

Why it's here

This is a multi-layered and brutal story that is bolstered by the strong performances from a perfect cast.




City of God (2002)

Gross Revenue: $30.60 million

Ratings:

8.6 / 10

91%

Directors

Fernando Meirelles, Katia Lund

Stars

Alexandre Rodrigues, Leandro Firmino, Jonathan Haagensen, Alice Braga

Synopsis

Two young boys growing up in a notorious slum in Rio de Janeiro take separate paths in life, one aspiring to photography and the other becoming a heartless kingpin. Rocket, the photographer, becomes entangled in the power clash between the powerful gangs of the city and captures the rise and fall of his childhood friend turned kingpin, Li'l Ze.

Why it's here

City of God portrays a chilling reality for the lives of kids who grow up in these brutal slums. It gives a compelling look at the triumphs and life-threatening shortcomings of these kids tempted as children by the lives they saw those older than them live.
