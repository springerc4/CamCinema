One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (1975)

Gross Revenue: $163.30 million

Ratings:

8.7 / 10

93%

Director

Milos Forman

Stars

Jack Nicholson, Louise Fletcher, Will Sampson

Synopsis

McMurphy (Jack Nicholson), a criminal pretending to be insane to try to get out of working in prison. He, instead, ends up in a mental hospital. Once there, he makes many wild attempts to rally the timid prisoners against the oppressive head nurse.

Why it's here

This is an adapatation of another one of my favorite novels, this one written by Ken Kesey. It is an in-depth commentary on authoritarianism and mental illness. This is one of Jack Nicholson's finest performances of his career, yet it does not overshadow the rest of the movie by any means.




Requiem for a Dream (2000)

Gross Revenue: $7.40 million

Ratings:

8.3 / 10

79%

Director

Darren Aronofsky

Stars

Jared Leto, Jennifer Connelly, Ellen Burstyn, Marlon Wayans

Synopsis

Requiem for a Dream ties together the stories of four characters struggling with drug addiction as they fall deeper into desperation to fulfill their evermore demanding lifestyle.

Why it's here

This is one of the most disturbing non-horror films of all time. By the end of the movie, it serves it's part as an extremely powerful anti-drug PSA and the heartbreaking situations the naive characters end up in pack a huge punch that will stick with you for a while after watching.




There Will Be Blood (2007)

Gross Revenue: $76.20 million

Ratings:

8.2 / 10

92%

Director

Paul Thomas Anderson

Stars

Daniel Day-Lewis, Paul Dano, Dillon Freasier

Synopsis

There Will Be Blood tells the competitive tale of Daniel Plainview, an oilman in the early days of Southern California's booming oil business. He crosses paths with Eli Sunday, a man whose brother agrees to let Daniel dig on their family farm. The tale of these two complicated men tells a searing tale of greed, religion, family, and revenge.

Why it's here

Paul Dano and Daniel-Day Lewis turn in two incredibly passionate performances. It is primarily the on-screen dispute between these two that carries the movie for me and makes it easy to return time and time again.
