2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)

Gross Revenue: $146 million

Ratings:

8.3 / 10

92%

Director

Stanley Kubrick

Stars

Keir Dullea, Gary Lockwood, William Sylvester

Synopsis

An artifact excavated on the moon which may be a sign of extraterrestrial life leads a crew through space to investigate. In the vast emptiness of space, the crew encounters a myriad of problems, including difficulties with the ship's supercomputer HAL 9000.

Why it's here

A monumental acheivement by director Stanley Kubrick, 2001 tells an evolutionary story of man in a way that nobody since has ever been able to emulate. The breathtaking effects, visceral loneliness, and the unforgettable Stargate sequence make this movie, in my opinion, one of the best ever made.

Synopsis

In a future world, a special unit utilize the superhuman cognitive abilities of three people called Pre-Cogs to stop crimes before they happen. The system begins to show a break from routine when one of the officers from that unit is predicted to commit a crime himself.
